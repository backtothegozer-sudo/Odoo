The official 11 March 2026 release marks a clear shift: enterprise AI is being framed as factory-grade production with stronger governance, security, and delivery discipline, all directly connected to sovereign AI execution requirements.

1. What is officially announced

NTT DATA unveiled NVIDIA-powered enterprise AI factories designed to accelerate deployment of AI use cases at scale. The core signal is not only compute performance; it is the ability to run production workflows under tighter security controls, operating standards, and governance expectations.

2. Why this matters for sovereign AI strategy

Sovereignty is not just about model location. It also depends on orchestration control, compliance evidence, operational resilience, and reversibility. The AI factory model is relevant because it pushes organizations away from ad hoc pilots and toward auditable, repeatable industrial execution.

3. What enterprises should launch now

Teams should map critical AI use cases, separate workflows that require higher data-control constraints, and define a target architecture baseline covering security, compliance, observability, and cost governance before scaling pilots further.
